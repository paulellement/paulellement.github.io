Frequently Asked Questions

Is the sky blue?

Sometimes.

When is the sky blue?

When the sky is cloudless and the sun has yet to fall below the edge of the world.

Why is the sky blue?

Earth's atmosphere scatters all light. However, it scatters higher energy light (i.e., the second half of the rainbow: green, blue, and purple) more than lower energy light (i.e., the first half of the rainbow: red, orange, and yellow). So, the former colours pass through the atmosphere more easily, hitting our eyes.

However, when the sun is about to fall below the edge of the world, the light must travel through more atmosphere, which gives the blue-ish colours a chance to scatter. So, we start to see the warm colours too! Note that green is a weak colour that is outperformed by blue-ish and red-ish colours, so we never see green in the sky. Thank the lord for chlorophyll, otherwise green (the best colour) would be rare!
